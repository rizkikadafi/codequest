Goals

Inclusive, sustainable and safe cities are one of the main focuses of the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) set by the United Nations (UN). Under Goal 11, cities and settlements around the world are required to achieve inclusive, safe and sustainable standards.

Goal 11 of the SDGs aims to create cities and human settlements that are inclusive, safe, resilient and sustainable. This includes efforts to increase access to adequate housing, efficient public transportation, green open spaces, as well as encouraging sustainable urban planning and better disaster management.

Sustainable Cities

Why They Matter?

More than 90% of COVID-19 infections are concentrated in urban areas, with the most severe impact felt by the 1 billion inhabitants of densely populated slums worldwide. Even prior to the pandemic, rapid urbanization had placed over half of the global population, equivalent to 4 billion people, in cities grappling with deteriorating air quality, insufficient infrastructure and services, and unregulated urban expansion. The successful containment of COVID-19 in some instances underscores the remarkable ability of urban communities to adapt to evolving circumstances.

Why Should I Care?

All of these challenges will inevitably impact each individual. Inequity may result in turmoil and instability, pollution negatively impacts the health of everyone and hinders the productivity of the workforce, thus impacting the overall economy, and natural disasters have the capacity to disrupt the lives of all.

What Can I Do?

Get involved in the administration and supervision of your urban area. Speak up for the type of city you think is necessary. Construct a plan for your residence, street, and local area, and put that plan into action. Are there sufficient employment opportunities? Is it secure for your children to walk to school? Can you take an evening stroll with your family? How accessible is public transportation? What is the state of air quality? What do the communal spaces look like? The more you enhance your community, the more significant the impact on your overall well-being.

Component of Sustainable Cities

Sustainable Infrastructure

Infrastructure that supports sustainable cities involves environmentally friendly technologies and facilities, such as renewable energy sources, efficient public transportation systems, and effective water management.

Sustainable Transportation

A good, affordable public transportation system helps reduce traffic congestion, greenhouse gas emissions, and congestion. It also improves accessibility for city residents.

Affordable Housing

Creating affordable housing for various levels of society is an important component. This includes housing that is energy efficient and close to public facilities.

Green Open Space

The availability of parks, city parks and other green open spaces is important for the well-being of city residents. These open spaces not only help clean the air but also provide a place for recreation and social gatherings.

Energy Savings

Encouraging efficient use of energy and renewable energy sources is a key component in sustainable cities. This includes the use of energy-saving lighting, good building insulation, and the use of solar or wind energy.
